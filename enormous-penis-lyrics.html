About

Da Vinci’s Notebook was an American comedy a cappella group that formed in 1993 and disbanded in 2004. The group consisted of four members: Paul Sabourin, Greg “Storm” DiCostanzo, Bernie Muller-Thym, and Richard Hsu. They were known for their humorous lyrics, intricate vocal harmonies, and performances that often incorporated comedic skits and parodies of popular songs.

Da Vinci’s Notebook released three studio albums during their career: “The Life and Times of Mike Fanning” (1998), “Brontosaurus” (2002), and “Bendy’s Law” (2003). Some of their most well-known songs include “Enormous Penis,” “Title of the Song,” and “Another Irish Drinking Song.”

Although they disbanded in 2004, the members have continued to work on various musical projects individually and together.
