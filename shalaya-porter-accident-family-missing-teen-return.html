Shalaya Porter Accident

The disappearance of Shalaya Porter on June 5, 2023, has raised concerns that she may have been involved in an accident. However, as of now, no information is available regarding any accident involving her.

According to her family, a teenager about to graduate from high school has gone missing in Philadelphia.

Shalaya,18 years old, was last seen on June 5 in the Mill Creek neighborhood on Fairmount Avenue, as reported by the Philadelphia Police Department.

The police released a statement on June 8 describing Shalaya, stating that she is approximately 5 feet 6 inches tall, weighs around 120 pounds, has a medium build, brown eyes, light brown complexion, black hair, and a nose piercing.

No specific details were given about her clothing at the time of her disappearance. It was mentioned that Shalaya is known to use public transportation, but her current whereabouts are unknown.

On the other hand, the missing girl’s family is distraught and believes that Shalaya did not voluntarily disappear.

Shalaya’s aunt, Felicia Hill, expressed concern, stating that Shalaya would never have missed her graduation or the opportunity to go to college and that she would never have left her mother.

Family members shared additional details with a local news outlet, WCAU. They mentioned that Shalaya’s phone was found left on her bed, her high school graduation took place last Friday, and she had an excellent attendance record at school.

They also mentioned that Shalaya had enrolled at Morgan State University to pursue studies in architecture, design, and fashion, with plans to start her studies in the upcoming fall semester.

Missing Teen Shalaya Porter Family Asks For Her Safe Return

Porter’s family is reaching out to the community, requesting assistance in any possible way. On Tuesday, a community gathering occurred at Motivation High School on 59th Street and Baltimore Avenue to aid the search.

During the gathering, friends and family distributed approximately 3,000 flyers to help spread awareness.

While fighting back the tears at the rally and search event, Porter’s mother, Kaleia Porter, described her daughter as a beautiful, incredible, thoughtful, and intelligent young lady, as reported by WCAU.

The family is determined and persistent in their search efforts, refusing to give up.

Hill, a family member, emphasized the importance of assistance through prayers and any relevant information, urging people to reach out and contact them, as mentioned on CBS News.

Expressing her heartfelt longing for her child’s return, Kaleia Porter simply stated, “I just want my baby home.”

She added, “I love you, baby, come home,” assuring her daughter they miss her and will overcome this challenging situation.
