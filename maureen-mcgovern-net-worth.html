FAQs About Maureen McGovern

Who is Maureen McGovern?

Maureen McGovern is an American singer and Broadway actress, best known for her hit song The Morning After from the 1972 film The Poseidon Adventure.

She has a career spanning several decades in music, theater, and television.

What are some of her biggest hits?

Her most notable songs include The Morning After, We May Never Love Like This Again from The Towering Inferno, and Different Worlds, the theme song from the TV show Angie.

What genre of music does she sing?

She primarily sings vocal and traditional pop music. Her voice has been praised for its technical brilliance and versatility.

Has she won any awards?

Yes, she has received a Grammy nomination and has won the Grand Prize award at the Tokyo Musical Festival. Additionally, she has several Gold Records in the US, Canada, Australia, Japan, and the Philippines.

What Broadway shows has she appeared in?

She has appeared in several Broadway productions, including Little Women where she played Marmee, and she has also performed in national tours of musicals.

Is she still performing?

No, she announced in 2022 that she would no longer perform in concerts due to her diagnosis with posterior cortical atrophy, a form of Alzheimer’s disease.

However, she continues to work on musical projects and advocate for musical therapy.

What albums has she released?

She has released numerous albums including The Morning After (1973), Nice to Be Around (1974), Baby I’m Yours (1992), Out of This World (1996), The Music Never Ends (1997), The Pleasure of His Company (1998), and A Long and Winding Road (2008).
