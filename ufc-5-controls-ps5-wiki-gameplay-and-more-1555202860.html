UFC 5 Controls PS5

In UFC 5 on the PS5, you'll need to master various controls for different aspects of the game, such as striking, grappling, and ground fighting. These controls can vary based on your fighting style and position in the match. Here's a simplified breakdown of the key controls:

Striking Controls:

  • Use the left stick to move your fighter.

  • To throw punches, press Square or Triangle buttons.

  • For kicks, use the X or Circle buttons.

  • Move the right stick to control your fighter's head movement.

  • Change your fighter's stance by pressing R3.

  • Taunt your opponent with the D-pad.

  • Hold L1 for technical modifications.

  • Hold L2 for body modifications.

  • Hold R1 for signature moves.

  • Hold R2 to block high.

  • Hold R2 and L2 together for a timed low block.

Grappling Controls:

  • For takedowns, tap Square, X, or Triangle while holding L2.

  • For more powerful takedowns, press and hold Square, X, or Triangle while holding L2.

  • To initiate a clinch, press X and Square together.

  • Defend against takedowns by holding L2 and R2.

  • Use the right stick for grappling in different directions.

Striking Controls in Clinch:

  • To deliver strikes in the clinch, use Square, Triangle, X, or Circle.

  • Use LB and RB to control your fighter's advanced movements.

Ground Controls:

  • When you're on the ground, use the left stick for grappling.

  • To transition and move on the ground, use the right stick.

  • To punch while on the ground, press Triangle or Square.

  • For kicks, use X or Circle.

  • Hold L1 for advanced ground and pound.

  • Hold L2 for body shots.

  • Hold R1 for regular ground and pound.

  • Hold R2 to block high.

  • Hold R2 and L2 together for a timed low block.

Defense Controls:

  • To get up, move the left stick upwards.

  • If you're attempting a submission, move the left stick to the left.

  • For ground and pound, move the left stick to the right.

  • To defend against transitions, hold R2 and use the right stick.

UFC 5

UFC 5 Gameplay

In EA SPORTS UFC 5, the gameplay takes a big leap forward with impressive graphics, a realistic damage system, and doctor stoppages for added authenticity. Your fighter's appearance changes based on the damage they give and take, with over 64,000 ways to show facial damage. This damage also affects their abilities in the game.

One exciting feature is that if your fighter gets seriously hurt, like a broken nose or swollen eye, the referee can pause the action and bring in a ringside doctor to check the damage. This adds a real sense of drama to the fights. New striking animations, such as ground and pound elbows, spinning attacks, and various punches and kicks, make the action feel more lifelike.

The grappling system has been improved with quicker moves and no more mini-games during submissions, making it smoother and more realistic. UFC 5 Controls PS5 - FAQs

1. When is EA Sports UFC 5 set to be released?  

EA Sports UFC 5 is scheduled for release on October 27, 2023, for the PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S consoles.

2. What's new in terms of gameplay features in UFC 5?  

UFC 5 brings enhanced graphics, a realistic damage system that affects your character's attributes, doctor stoppages, and an array of new striking animations for a more authentic fighting experience.

3. How does the authentic damage system work in UFC 5?  

In the game, cuts, bruises, and swelling accumulate based on the damage your fighter gives and takes. This can lead to over 64,000 possible combinations of facial damage that impact your fighter's abilities.

4. What are doctor stoppages, and how do they affect gameplay?  

Doctor stoppages are introduced to provide a realistic outcome. If your fighter sustains significant injuries, like a broken nose or swollen eye, the referee can temporarily halt the match, and a ringside doctor examines the damage, adding a sense of drama to the fights.

5.  Can you tell me more about the new striking animations in UFC 5?  

UFC 5 features new animations for ground and pound elbows, spinning attacks, and various punches, kicks, knees, and elbows. These animations mimic the moves of different fighters, making the action more lifelike.
