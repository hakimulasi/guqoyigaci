Early Life & Biography

Christopher Judge was born on October 13, 1964, in Los Angeles in California. His younger brother Jeff also is an actor. From a very young age, Christopher inclined the acting and studied drama in high school. He had a feeling that his career in sports might act as a stepping stone into the world of acting. While in high school, Christopher became an All-L.A. A city football player at Carson High in 1982.

Christopher used to play under the legendary coach Gene Vollnogle. Christopher then enrolled in the University of Oregon after receiving a football scholarship and played as safety and defensive back for the Ducks from 1982 to 1985. He used to lead the kick off return yardage for 1983 to 1984 and the interceptions in 1984.

Christopher received the Casanova award in 1982, even though he was a freshman in the college. He was selected in Pacific-10 Conference and played the Hula Bowl of 1985. He won an opportunity to host a radio show in his senior year on the West Coast FOX KLSR Morning Show. He decided to use this opportunity to move to LA and get an agent.

Career

Christopher began to study at the Howard Fine Studio in LA in 1989. Over the next few years, he made numerous appearances in movies in small roles. Few of his notable appearances include 21 Jump Street, The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air, House Party 2, and on the show Sirens quite often, which aired from 1994 to 1995. His most popular casting was in Stargate SG-1.

His other works include Andromeda, Stargate Atlantis, the Mentalist, Personal Effects, Snow Dogs, and A Dog’s Breakfast. He appeared on NCIS: Los Angeles and in the infamous 2012 film The Dark Knight Rises. As a writer, he wrote four of Stargate SG-1 episodes, which include “The Warrior” in season five, “The Changeling” in season six,” Birthright” in season seven, and “Sacrifice” in season eight.

Christopher also worked as a writer on the show Rage of Angels. He is famous for his voice acting performances as well. His works include the voice over for Piccolo in Dragonball: Evolution, Magneto in X-Men: Evolution, and Stargate Alliance, which got canceled later on. He was also a part of Def Jam Vendetta, Def Jam: Fight for New York and Turok. He became the voice actor for Kratos in God of War on June 14, 2016.

Awards & Achievements

In 2012 he was nominated for a Saturn Award for best-supporting actor for his role in Stargate SG-1. He received the D.I.C.E Award in 2019 for outstanding achievement in character for his work in God of War. He was nominated for the British Academy Games Award for Performer.
